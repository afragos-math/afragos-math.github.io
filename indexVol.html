Important note: The forthcoming death of volunteerism...

Φύγε κι άσε με μοναχό, που βλέπω να πληθαίνει
απάνω η νύχτα, και βαθιά να γίνονται τα χάη.
Ούτε του πόνου η θύμηση σε λίγο πια δε μένει,
κι είμαι άνθος που φυλλοροεί στο χέρι σου και πάει

Φύγε καθώς τα χρόνια κείνα εφύγανε, που μόνον
μια λέξη σου ήταν, στη ζωή, για μένα σαν παιάνας.
Τώρα τα χείλη μου διψούν τα φίλημα της μάνας,
της μάνας γης, και ανοίγοντας στο γέλιο των αιώνων.

Φύγε, η καρδιά μου νοσταλγεί την άπειρη γαλήνη!
Ταράζει και η ανάσα σου τα μαύρα της Στυγός
νερά, που με πηγαίνουν, όπως είμαι ναυαγός,
εκεί, στο απόλυτο Μηδέν, στην Απεραντοσύνη.

Κώστας Καρυωτάκης.

翻訳:

Go and leave me alone, I see the night increasing above
and the abysses growing deeper.
Not even the memory of pain remains for long now,
and I am a flower that sheds its leaves in your hand and is gone.

Go, as those years have gone which only
one word of yours was, in life, for me like a paean.
Now my lips thirst for the mother's kiss,
the mother's, mother Earth's, and opening to the laughter of the ages.

Go, my heart longs for infinite calm!
Your breath disturbs even the black waters of Styx,
waters that carry me, as I am, shipwrecked,
there, to the absolute Zero, into the Vastness.

Translation

The principle from which we proceed is the simplest possible: Knowledge is a good and not a commodity, and as such it should be provided to a large extent free of charge. In particular, the public character of the university defends freedom itself, since it promotes equality, strongly shifts power towards the people and equips them not only with knowledge, but with the sound judgment that allows for intellectual exchange and the strong expression of just reasoning. We are of course only a small cog in the broader system, but we are particularly happy that in our own way we have managed to contribute to the free nature of education and bring students together through our common purpose.

Now society is changing, and with it the Greek university is being dragged along. Public institutions are being degraded in favor of the private sector, backed by almost provocative legistlation, which we will not analyse at this point of time. What concern us here is the conclusion of these actions, with which we interact on a regular basis.

The number of students enrolling each year has plummeted and interest is fading, and now that the number of people fighting for knowledge can be counted on the fingers of one hand, we feel alone. Personally, struggling with depression and listening to my conscience calling out to me to stop wallowing in vanity, I withdrew from volunteering to a large extent. What is the point of all this, while there is a method of organised dissolution? This breaks my heart, as the act of offering for the greater good was my refuge (and, to be honest, one of my very few ways to feel somewhat valuable). I imagine a large number of students think the same way and regret the situation. We ended up broken up and scattered, which is exactly what ultimately shouldn't have happened.

The volunteer effort must remain alive for as long as possible. Not because I believe it can be saved, because I really don't, but because that's how our message is transmitted, that we remain fighting and that freedom is never left undefended. And when we eventually lose, we will know that we will have kept the ashes warm enough for the next phoenixes to sprout.
