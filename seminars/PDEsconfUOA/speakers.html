Particles almost in contact: the method of reduced functionals in 3D

Université libre de Bruxelles

The formal analysis of the forces and torques on two moving solid particles suspended in a laminar flow and almost in contact with each other (or on a particle almost in contact with the wall of a container) traces back at least to Brenner and Cox in the late 1960’s by using lubrication theory. While the stream function is defined up to a constant in 2D, the vector potential in 3D is defined up to a gradient and the choice of a gauge. I will show that by choosing an ad-hoc gauge, one can find the optimal potential by solving the dual formulation of a resulting Euler-Lagrange equation [...]
